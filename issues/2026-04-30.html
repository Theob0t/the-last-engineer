Editor's Note

The Cursor SDK launch caught me off guard — most companies would ship a glorified wrapper and call it programmatic access. Instead they built a proper TypeScript interface for automating code workflows. That's infrastructure thinking.

OpenAI's goblin postmortem is fascinating for what it reveals about tuning fragility. One bad batch in their fine-tuning pipeline corrupted the model personality across multiple deployments. We're still flying blind on instruction-following reliability.

The search stack piece asks the right question: can agents replace traditional search? The answer is "not yet" but for interesting reasons. LLMs can't evaluate their own knowledge gaps. That's not a training problem — it's architectural.
