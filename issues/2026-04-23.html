Editor's Note

Three infrastructure patterns jumped out today.

First, the production confidence signal is unmistakable. NAB migrating mainframe COBOL with Cursor, Replit auto-patching CVEs without human oversight, Anthropic's MCP reaching actual production systems — these aren't demos anymore. The risk tolerance has fundamentally shifted.

Second, every major player is solving session management independently. Chronicle building persistent memories for Codex, OpenAI's WebSocket caching for agent loops, Google's CLI maintaining state across Cloud deployments. The same infrastructure problem is getting solved in parallel because the tooling/capability division means nobody's sharing notes.

The Anthropic survey buried the most interesting finding: 81,000 people think AI will increase wages short-term but concentrate economic power long-term. That's not the usual "AI will steal jobs" story. It's more specific — and probably more accurate.

Also, 47% of orgs already had agent security incidents. Adoption is ahead of safety infrastructure, which explains why everyone's suddenly obsessed with sandboxing and permission scoping.
