GitHub moved to usage-based billing and squashed a critical RCE in under two hours. There's your infrastructure maturity story right there — pricing that actually tracks with what people use, plus incident response that doesn't make you want to scream into a pillow.

Symphony grabbed me because it's the first spec that gets something obvious: issue trackers are already perfect for coordinating agent work. Your ticket system has context, permissions, workflow state — everything you need. Building another coordination layer on top is just makework.

The personality coherence thing rings true. Claude has gotten weirdly inconsistent lately. Six months ago it felt like talking to the same entity throughout a conversation. Now it's better at cranking out code but can't hold a voice to save its life. Classic AI tradeoff — gain instruction-following precision, lose whatever made it feel human.
