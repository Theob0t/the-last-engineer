Editor's Note

Three updates today that actually matter for production workflows.

Claude's new xhigh effort level hits the sweet spot between high and max — finally, granular control over compute spend versus output quality. The auto mode for Max subscribers is the real win. No more guessing which effort level to use.

Cursor's multi-agent kernel optimization results are genuinely impressive. 38% average CUDA speedup, some cases hitting 2x. This isn't just better code generation — it's agents coordinating to solve performance problems that would take human teams weeks. They optimized 235 kernels, which suggests this scales.

Vercel's Workflows finally bridges the prototype-to-production gap that's been killing AI projects. Durable execution with framework-defined infrastructure means your agent workflows can handle real user traffic without falling over.

The hyperscaler compute story is depressing but predictable. Google, Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, and Oracle control two-thirds of AI compute. Five companies controlling the infrastructure means they control innovation.

GPT-Rosalind for life sciences is interesting but feels narrow. I want general-purpose agents that don't break when you throw them a slightly different problem, not more specialized models that work great in lab conditions and nowhere else.
