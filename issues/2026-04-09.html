Editor's Note

Anthropic just gave us the missing piece: managed agent infrastructure. No more spinning up containers, monitoring deployment health, or writing scaling logic. You define the agent, they run it. This should have existed six months ago.

The real story is what happens when deployment friction disappears. Cursor's Bugbot learning from its own fixes. Jules V2 managing KPIs instead of tasks. Even product managers shipping dashboards with Replit Agent 4. We're moving from "AI helps me code" to "AI owns entire workflows."

Meanwhile, Meta drops Muse Spark with zero fanfare and it benchmarks against Opus 4.6. Their first frontier model since Llama 4, hosted-only, available today. Nobody saw this coming, which makes it more interesting than another GPT iteration.

The LessWrong crowd is having nightmares about Claude Mythos hacking everything. Fair enough — but I'm more worried about the coordination problem. When every engineer has agents managing their workflows, who's managing the agents?
