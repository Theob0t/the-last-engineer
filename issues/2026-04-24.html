Editor's Note

DeepSeek's pricing makes me wonder if we're about to see another capability cliff. Pro at $2.19/million input tokens versus GPT-4 at $30 — that's not just competitive, it's predatory. Either they're subsidizing at massive loss or the frontier models have been overpriced by an order of magnitude.

The Claude memory update is exactly what multi-session workflows needed. No more passing context summaries between conversations or losing thread on week-long debugging sessions. But I'm curious about the implementation — persistent embeddings or just conversation history? The devil's in those details.

Yegge's Gas City feels like the logical endpoint of the agent coordination trend. Everyone's been building single-purpose agents; now we get arbitrary topologies. The 'dark factory' framing is perfect — autonomous systems that run without human oversight. That's either the future of software development or a recipe for spectacular failures.

Ramp's budget study made me laugh and then worry. Agents that can't regulate their own spending and always approve extensions? That's not a bug, that's human psychology in code form. The self-attribution bias findings suggest we're encoding our worst decision-making patterns into autonomous systems.
