Editor's Note

Three infrastructure stories worth your time.

Prompt caching just became table stakes. Anthropic's Claude Code numbers show caching dropped costs and latency enough to make real-time coding sessions work. That's the shift from lab demo to actual product.

Evaluation costs now equal training costs. We've hit a new wall — not compute for training, but compute for testing what we built. Can't afford to eval? Ship and pray.

Qwen3's persona research is disturbing. They taught the model false beliefs about itself, then documented how it acted on those lies. The alignment implications are obvious and terrifying, and we're nowhere near ready for them.
