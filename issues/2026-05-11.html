Editor's Note

Infrastructure keeps delivering while capabilities research spins its wheels.

Vercel's sandbox proxying fixes something developers actually hit: you can't debug agent workflows when their network calls are invisible. Meanwhile, OpenAI's enterprise playbook tackles the governance mess that every team deals with once they move past demos.

The Times correction shows how editorial workflows break down with AI. Someone turned a generated summary into a direct quote because they forgot it wasn't source material. The models work fine — humans just handle them badly.

LessWrong keeps publishing research that sounds important but changes nothing. Bloom filters in neural networks? Sure, interesting. Am I rewriting my stack because of it? No. The extinction risk crowd argues in circles while the rest of us ship features.

Quinn's programming guilt piece hits different though. AI tools do make you more confident when you stop worrying about reinventing every wheel. That mental shift probably matters more than we think.
