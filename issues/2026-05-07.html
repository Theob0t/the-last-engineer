Editor's Note

The infrastructure is finally catching up to the capabilities.

Cursor autoinstalls dependencies so you don't break flow. Windsurf embeds review directly in workspace. Claude adds orchestration between multiple agents. These aren't flashy capability jumps — they're the boring plumbing that makes agentic workflows actually work in production.

Meanwhile, we're getting the first real data on what enterprises do with AI agents at scale. OpenAI's B2B research and the Anthropic-SpaceX compute deal reveal something interesting: the companies winning aren't the ones with the fanciest models. They're the ones who figured out workflow integration first.

Simon's piece on vibe coding converging with agentic engineering hits the real shift. Manual and autonomous approaches are meeting in the middle — you sketch the direction, agents handle the tedious parts, you course-correct. That feedback loop is getting tighter every week.

One number that stuck: computer use costs 45x more than APIs, but teams still use it because building proper integrations is expensive. We'll pay the compute premium to avoid the engineering overhead. For now.
