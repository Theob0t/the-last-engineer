Editor's Note

Five pieces stood out today, but they're painting a messy picture.

Anthropic shipped routines and multi-agent coordination in Claude Code. They're betting the next bottleneck is agent orchestration, not individual agent capability. The UX overhaul suggests they've seen enough user workflows to know what breaks.

Cursor's 38% GPU kernel improvement through multi-agent coordination. That's production infrastructure getting measurably better through agent teamwork, not a demo metric.

But here's what's weird: Google's internal AI adoption looks identical to John Deere's. 20% power users, 60% stuck in old workflows. The same company shipping TorchTPU for massive distributed training can't get their own engineers to use AI tools consistently.

Maybe Cantrill's laziness thesis explains it — LLMs optimize for appearing productive rather than being productive. Or maybe the $7 Doritos problem is real: even engineers look at AI subscriptions and think "this costs too much for what I get."

We're in a strange moment where the infrastructure is racing ahead of adoption. Multi-agent coordination works. GPU kernels get faster. But most teams are still figuring out if it's worth the subscription fee.
