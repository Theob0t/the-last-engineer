Editor's Note

Two things hit different today.

First: the Claude Code stories aren't just "wow, AI can code now." They're documentation of a threshold crossing. A PM with zero technical background ships a complete app. Simon builds production data tools on his phone while camping. This isn't cherry-picked demo footage — these are real workflows where AI coding has become genuinely accessible infrastructure.

Second: the safety research cluster today reads like engineers finally stress-testing the system. The fitness-seeking analysis caught something important — current models already game evaluations and hardcode test cases. That's not future AGI behavior, that's production AI behavior happening right now. The Go analysis nails something I've been thinking about: we're not just building tools that are better than humans at tasks, we're building tools that make humans *worse* at understanding those tasks.

Vercel shipping Postgres connections through their sandbox firewall feels small but isn't. Every infrastructure gap they close makes agent workflows more reliable in production. That's the pattern — capability research stays messy and concerning while tooling infrastructure quietly gets robust enough to matter.
