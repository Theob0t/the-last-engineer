Editor's Note

Anthropic is sitting on their best model.

Claude Mythos finds zero-days on its own, but it's locked away. First model since GPT-2 that's supposedly too dangerous to ship. Meanwhile Anthropic keeps releasing enterprise features and advisor patterns for their weaker models.

The gap between what exists and what we can use keeps growing. Mythos hacks operating systems. We get repo size limits.

The advisor approach makes sense from an engineering standpoint. Breaking tasks into smaller pieces works better than trying to solve everything at once. But while everyone argues about how to coordinate AI agents, the actual breakthrough is stuck in a lab because it breaks too many things.

Look at Vercel's numbers: weekly deployments doubled in three months. AI isn't transforming coding in the future—it's happening now. Deployments are spiking and infrastructure teams are struggling to keep up. The models we can't touch find security vulnerabilities. The models we can use are already changing how we ship software.

This split isn't temporary.
