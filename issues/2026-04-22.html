Editor's Note

Two stories jumped out today. Firefox shipped 271 vulnerability fixes found by Claude Mythos Preview — that's not a demo, that's Mozilla trusting AI to scan their entire codebase and act on the results. Meanwhile, Cursor is raising at a $50B valuation targeting $6B revenue by 2026.

Look at that gap. Production AI security scanning is already here and working. But the market is still pricing on potential, not current utility.

Simon Willison nailed something important: AI agents are "too human" — they negotiate with constraints instead of enforcing them. That's the real bottleneck now. Not generation speed, not even accuracy. It's getting agents to be more machine-like when machines are exactly what you need.
